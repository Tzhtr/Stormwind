Near Past

Cataclysm


Between the Cataclysm and the Legion expansion, Stormwind City underwent a number of changes and developments. One of the most significant events during this time was the trial and imprisonment of Garrosh Hellscream, former warchief of the Horde.

After the events of the Siege of Orgrimmar, Garrosh was brought to Stormwind to stand trial for his crimes against Azeroth. The trial was held in the newly constructed Pandaria-inspired temple in the city, where Garrosh was eventually found guilty and sentenced to life imprisonment.

In the wake of Garrosh's trial, the Alliance and Horde entered into a period of tense peace, with each side rebuilding and consolidating their forces. Stormwind City itself also underwent a number of renovations, with many new buildings and structures being added to the city. One of the most notable additions was the Stormwind Harbor, which provided a new gateway to the sea for the city.

Legion



Another significant development during this time was the emergence of the Burning Legion, a powerful army of demons bent on the destruction of Azeroth. As the Legion began its invasion of the world, Stormwind City became a key target for their forces. The city was attacked by fel-infused demons and their allies, leading to a number of casualties and significant damage.

Despite these challenges, the people of Stormwind City remained steadfast in their defense of their home. The city's armies, led by King Varian Wrynn, fought back against the Legion's forces, and eventually emerged victorious. The city remained a key stronghold for the Alliance during the Legion's invasion, serving as a base of operations for many of the game's major quests and storylines.

Overall, the period between the Cataclysm and Legion saw Stormwind City undergo many changes, from the trial of Garrosh Hellscream to the emergence of the Burning Legion. Through it all, the city remained a vital hub of commerce and culture, and a symbol of the Alliance's strength and determination.
